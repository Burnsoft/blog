iPrefs to App Swipe to Review to Nowhere….

Dec 20th, 2011

“App reviews fine” - me, (2009-yesterday)

“App review sucks” - me, (this morning)

“App review fine” - me, (this evening)

These past few weeks i’ve been working on a new app that uses the Notification Center (pull down menu) in iOS 4+ to launch other apps and perform daily tasks (compose iMessage, Tweet, mail etc).

iPrefs

It started life as an app called ‘iPrefs’ and the catalyst was the Settings URL scheme that became public knowledge at the iOS5 release. (e.g. prefs:root=AIRPLANE_MODE)

It made launching your settings easy, bluetooth was one swipe and a click away, as was airplane mode. It was fully localized in French, German, Italian and Spanish, would be free with one saved notification shortcut (in-app purchase for max of 10), worked great and a really nice looking app very similiar to the notification center.

Before I submitted to Apple for review I checked the Store for any other apps with similiar functionality and found one other, Switches+ (still available as at time of writing), so thought that the prefs URL’s were ok to use. The app was uploaded on the 25th November, and rejected on the 2nd December. ‘None public URL scheme’ was the reason for rejection. (I had other bugs to fix, but on subsequent resolution center queries was made aware that under no circumstances could I use the prefs: urls. Even to help a user add a Twitter account when non was available in iOS5)

OK, fair enough. I understand that URL’s can change in the future, just move on I thought. So I did.

App Swipe

I still had this brilliant use for Notification Center (I now know that I wasn’t alone, @drbarnard @artysx @skorebrits were all working on similiar apps around this time) and decided to use the future functionality for iPrefs I had in mind as the basis for the new app. I didn’t have a name yet, so iPrefs was my working title.

It has been my first experience working on an app that includes a feature Apple believes falls fowl of their review guidelines.
